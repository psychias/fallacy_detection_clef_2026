On the encoder side, RoBERTa-large with synthetic contrastive augmentation reached competitive results across all six (subtask, track) cells, with pseudo-labelling unlocking the enhanced track for ST2 and ST3. On the LLM side, the CoT-LoRA configurations we evaluated did not show a validated benefit over the encoder within our compute envelope, though the 7B 5-fold standard deviation leaves that comparison underpowered rather than closed. Augmentation gains were modest but consistent (+0.014 to +0.087 across cells), and pseudo-labelling added the largest enhanced-track lift where it was applicable. The practical-external (\textsf{pe}) ST3 class continued to limit macro-F1 after targeted augmentation, threshold calibration, and a basis-axis meta-learner. We remain cautious about the enhanced-track numbers in particular: the rewriter's label-aware design makes them easier to obtain than to defend, and we report them only alongside the base-track and 5-fold CV anchors. Touch\'{e} continues to be a productive platform for benchmarking argument-aware NLP, and we look forward to further contributions in future editions.
