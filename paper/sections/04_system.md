# 4. System Description

## 4.1 Pipeline overview

[FIGURE 1: Training and inference pipeline for the submitted RoBERTa-large systems. Real, synthetic, and (for ST2/ST3 enhanced) pseudo-labelled examples are mixed at a per-stream loss weight sw into a single fine-tuning loop; at inference time the trained classifier head is decoded with per-class thresholds for ST3 and default argmax for ST1/ST2. The leakage audit gate (exp_0081) sits between pseudo-label generation and pseudo-label training, and is the only control flow that depends on the enhanced-track diagnostic. Per-stream loss weights are sw=1.0 (real), sw=0.5 (synthetic), and sw=0.7 (pseudo-labelled), reflecting our calibration of the synthetic and pseudo signals against the real signal.]

The same pipeline produces all six submitted runs, with per-cell hyperparameters summarised in Appendix A. Real examples are concatenated from the published Reddit fields (4.3), augmented with a small batch of LLM-generated synthetic contrastive pairs (4.4), and — for ST2 and ST3 enhanced — extended with pseudo-labelled unlabelled examples (4.5) once a leakage audit gate has passed. The three input streams (real, synthetic, pseudo-labelled) are interleaved in each training mini-batch and reweighted by per-stream loss weights sw: real examples carry weight 1.0, synthetic carry 0.5, and pseudo-labelled carry 0.7. The mixed corpus is fed into a RoBERTa-large encoder [Liu et al. 2019] with a task-specific classification head; loss is class-weighted cross-entropy for ST2 and ST3 (with class weights set to inverse-frequency on the dev partition) and unweighted cross-entropy for ST1. We use the HuggingFace transformers library throughout [Wolf et al. 2020], with the AdamW optimiser, a linear warmup over the first 10% of steps, and a peak learning rate of 1e-5. Inference for ST1 and ST2 uses default argmax over class logits; ST3 uses a per-class threshold sweep on the development partition (4.7). The same training loop is used for all six (subtask, track) cells; only the input fields, the loss weights, the truncation length, and the presence or absence of pseudo-labelled examples vary across cells.

## 4.2 Backbone selection

We compared RoBERTa-base, RoBERTa-large, DeBERTa-v3-base, DeBERTa-v3-large, and ModernBERT-large under matched data and training budgets. RoBERTa-large outperformed RoBERTa-base on the four (subtask, track) cells where we have both numbers: 0.72 vs. 0.69 on ST1 base (exp_0013 vs. exp_0004), 0.90 vs. 0.89 on ST1 enhanced (exp_0022 vs. exp_0016), and 0.71 vs. 0.66 on ST2 base (exp_0014 vs. exp_0005). The one cell where RoBERTa-base was marginally ahead — ST2 enhanced, 0.925 (exp_0017) vs. 0.923 (exp_0023) — is inside seed-variance for these models and we treat it as a tie rather than evidence against the larger backbone. We therefore standardised on RoBERTa-large for the six submitted runs.

DeBERTa-v3-large did not survive the fine-tuning recipes we tried. Plain dev runs collapsed to near-chance F1: exp_0007 reached only 0.35 on ST1 base before exceeding our wall-clock budget, exp_0008 collapsed to 0.030 on ST2 base, and exp_0009 and a re-run exp_0040 both stuck at 0.21 on ST3 base. Adding the synthetic batch triggered NaN explosions in mixed-precision training: exp_0041 produced 51 NaN-loss batches before we killed the run, and exp_0042, exp_0043, exp_0044 reproduced the same pathology on the other (subtask, sw) combinations. Switching to bf16 with a much smaller learning rate of 5e-6 (exp_0049, exp_0050, exp_0051) stopped the NaNs but produced near-zero F1, so the model was learning nothing useful at that recipe either. The aggregate picture is four NaN-explosion incidents and seven collapse-or-fail runs across DeBERTa-v3-large; after the eleventh, we removed it from the backbone shortlist.

ModernBERT-large [Warner et al. 2024] ran cleanly but did not beat RoBERTa-large on ST3 enhanced, reaching 0.72 (exp_0086) against RoBERTa-large's 0.74 (exp_0073). A 1.6 pp gap on a 4-way task is real but not enough to override the simpler, well-understood RoBERTa pipeline for a notebook-paper submission. For a corpus of roughly 473 ST3 examples, RoBERTa-large is the rational choice; we revisit the LLM alternatives in 4.6 and 6.1.

## 4.3 Input construction

We concatenate the available text fields into a single input separated by the standard RoBERTa special tokens `<s>...</s></s>...</s>`. For the base track the order is text_raw, text_raw_parent, text_raw_title, text_base, argument_base; for the enhanced track we append text_enhanced and argument_enhanced. Tokeniser truncation is set to max_len=384 for ST3 (matching the configurations of exp_0069 and exp_0073) and max_len=256 for ST1 and ST2 (matching exp_0033, exp_0035, and exp_0032). The choice between 256 and 384 was driven by the token-length diagnostic in exp_0061, which showed that ST3 inputs benefited measurably from the longer window once argument_enhanced was concatenated, while ST1/ST2 inputs fit comfortably inside 256 with only a small tail of truncations.

## 4.4 Synthetic data augmentation

We generated synthetic contrastive pairs using a frontier LLM via API. For each target label, we prompted the model with three real in-domain examples and asked it to produce a paired contrast — a minimally edited rewrite that flips the label while preserving topic, register, and Reddit-conversational style. Generations were filtered by a length sanity check and a string-overlap heuristic against the source pair to discourage trivial copies. The three batches are: synth_v002 (130 pairs for ST1/ST2, exp_0025; 110 pairs for ST3, exp_0030), synth_v003 (rare-class top-up for ST3, exp_0056: 100 epistemic-external + 50 practical-external pairs), and the pe-targeted batch (exp_0103: 100 practical-external + practical-anchor pairs). The exact prompt template and the generated pairs are released alongside this notebook in the TIRA submission package.

At a loss weight of sw=0.5, synth gave a consistent improvement over the real-only baselines: +0.018 F1 on ST1 base (exp_0013 0.72 to exp_0033 0.73), +0.015 on ST1 enhanced (exp_0022 0.90 to exp_0035 0.91), +0.019 on ST2 base (exp_0014 0.71 to exp_0032 0.73), and +0.014 on ST2 enhanced (exp_0023 0.92 to exp_0036 0.94). On ST3 enhanced, however, synth alone did not move the needle: the real-only baseline exp_0024 (F1 0.694) and the +synth configuration exp_0067 (also 0.694) tied, and the eventual gain on this cell only appeared once pseudo-labelling was added (4.5). The largest single gain came on ST3 base, where synth_v002 at max_len=384 lifted F1 from 0.44 (exp_0058) to 0.52 (exp_0069), an improvement of +0.087. Synth is not a free lunch: adding the synth_v003 rare-class top-up on top of synth_v002 dropped ST3-base F1 by 0.045 relative to synth_v002 alone (exp_0068 0.48 vs. exp_0069 0.52), and a focal-loss recipe [Lin et al. 2017] (exp_0053) lost 0.018 over the same baseline. We read this as confirmation of the general caveat documented by Li et al. [2023]: synthetic data improves the average and harms the worst-case, and which side you land on depends on the recipe.

## 4.5 Pseudo-labelling

For ST2 and ST3 enhanced we additionally added pseudo-labelled examples drawn from the published unlabelled pool. Predictions from a strong intermediate checkpoint were filtered by confidence and added to the training mix at a loss weight sw=0.7. Following the modern transformer-era treatment by Mukherjee and Awadallah [2020] and the original pseudo-label idea of Lee [2013], we treated this step with caution: pseudo-labelling on enhanced-track inputs interacts directly with the leakage concern of Section 3.2, so we ran a dedicated leak audit gate (exp_0081) before letting pseudo examples into training; the audit returned no detectable leak under the test we ran and we proceeded. The recipe paid off most clearly on ST2 enhanced, where adding pseudo lifted F1 from 0.94 (exp_0036) to 0.97 (exp_0072), a gain of +0.033 over the synth-only baseline. On ST3 enhanced the same recipe at max_len=384 lifted F1 from the synth_v002-only configuration to 0.74 (exp_0073), a gain of +0.041 over the closest matching baseline. The pseudo recipe at max_len=256 (exp_0071) underperformed its synth-only counterpart on ST1 enhanced (0.906 against 0.914, a loss of 0.008), which is one of the reasons we did not adopt pseudo for ST1 enhanced in the final submission.

## 4.6 The LLM detour

Encoders did not look like the obvious choice at the start of the project. Following the published precedent of Pan et al. [2024] — whose zero-shot LLM results are reported on different fallacy corpora and are therefore not directly numerically comparable — we ran a zero-shot Qwen2.5-3B baseline (exp_0091) on ST3 enhanced, which reached F1 0.47 — well below the RoBERTa baselines of 4.2. A kNN few-shot variant retrieving over a bge-large-en-v1.5 sentence embedding index (exp_0138, 1876x1024) recovered some ground at 0.53 (exp_0143), but still trailed RoBERTa-large by a wide margin. We then trained CoT-LoRA recipes [Hu et al. 2022; Dettmers et al. 2023; Wei et al. 2022] on Qwen2.5-7B and Qwen2.5-32B. The 7B 5-fold CV on ST3 enhanced reached 0.85 ± 0.11 (exp_0180); the 32B single-run reached 0.84 (exp_0207), but the corresponding 5-fold CV was still running at the submission deadline. We also evaluated Qwen3-4B-Thinking [Qwen Team 2025]: 5-fold CV reached 0.96 on ST2 enhanced (exp_0217), which is competitive with the submitted encoder, but on ST3 base the same model reached only 0.45 (exp_0218), below the 0.52 we already had with RoBERTa-large + synth_v002. Section 5.5 explains how the deadline-stale Qwen-32B CV interacted with our submission choice.

## 4.7 Threshold calibration for ST3 pe

A first diagnostic on ST3 used an NLI-style basis probe (exp_0082) whose per-class breakdown surfaced a clear weakness: pe F1 was 0.25, well below the other three classes. That number was what motivated the pe-targeted synth batch in 4.4. After training the final ST3-enhanced classifier, we ran a per-class threshold sweep on the dev partition (exp_0145); the best configuration — {ee=0.1, ei=0.1, pe=0.05, pi=0.4} — added +1.0 pp F1 over default argmax. A subsequent attempt to learn a basis-axis meta-learner from LLM signals (exp_0146) gave no improvement over the encoder-only weighted average and was not used in the submission. We document the negative meta-learner result here because it is part of the same investigation, and because the pe ceiling it failed to break is itself a finding (6.3).
