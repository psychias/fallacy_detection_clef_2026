We described a notebook-paper submission to the Touch\'{e} 2026 Fallacy Detection lab built around a fine-tuned RoBERTa-large encoder with small-scale LLM-generated synthetic augmentation and, for the enhanced track on ST2 and ST3, pseudo-labelled unlabelled examples. On our dev partition the six submitted runs reach F1-macro between 0.52 (ST3 base) and 0.97 (ST2 enhanced), with the enhanced-track numbers presented under the explicit leakage caveat of \S\ref{sec:disc-leakage}. Augmentation gains were modest but consistent (+0.014 to +0.087 across cells), and pseudo-labelling added the largest enhanced-track lift where it was applicable. The LLM tracks we tried did not outperform the encoder under cross-validation at this dataset scale, and the practical-external (\textsf{pe}) ST3 class continued to limit macro-F1 after targeted augmentation, threshold calibration, and a basis-axis meta-learner. We hope the per-recipe ablation, the backbone comparison including the DeBERTa failures and the LLM detour, and the explicit leakage discussion are useful to teams reusing this corpus in future editions of the lab.
