# 2. Background and Related Work

## 2.1 Fallacy detection in NLP

The Reddit informal-fallacy dataset that the Touché 2026 lab redistributes was introduced by Sahai et al. [2021], who labelled a long-tail eight-way taxonomy over comments mined from r/changemyview and adjacent subreddits and reported the first transformer baselines on the resulting corpus. Adjacent benchmarks treat closely related phenomena with similar fine-grained label structures. The Argument Reasoning Comprehension task introduced multi-class warrant identification on user-written argument pairs and surfaced how brittle large language models can be on argumentation tasks that require implicit-premise reasoning [Habernal et al. 2018]. Da San Martino et al. [2019] catalogued eighteen propaganda techniques in news articles; several of those techniques (appeal to authority, appeal to fear, black-and-white fallacy) overlap directly with the ST2 inventory and indicate that the propaganda-detection literature is a useful cross-domain reference even though the source genre differs. Goffredo et al. [2022] classified fallacies in political-debate transcripts and are the most direct lineage of the ST2 classifier we extend; their reported difficulty in distinguishing hasty generalisation from appeal to population mirrors what we observed in our own confusion structure.

Two more recent papers anchor the picture. Alhindi et al. [2022] reported the strongest prior multitask instruction-prompted results on the Sahai et al. data, demonstrating that multitask supervision over related fallacy datasets transfers usefully to the eight-way Reddit task. MAFALDA [Helwe et al. 2024] is the most-cited 2024 unified fallacy benchmark; we do not retrain on MAFALDA, but our ST2 label inventory is recognisable in its taxonomy and the MAFALDA error-analysis findings on rare-class behaviour echo what we report for the ST3 pe class in Section 6.3. Pan et al. [2024] provide the published precedent for evaluating LLMs as zero-shot fallacy classifiers; they show that zero-shot LLM performance is real but uneven across fallacy types, which motivates the LLM track we describe in Section 4.6 as worth running rather than dismissing.

## 2.2 Argumentation schemes

The four ST3 labels are not an ad-hoc taxonomy; they sit inside the Walton-Reed-Macagno scheme catalogue [Walton, Reed, Macagno 2008], which has been the canonical taxonomic backbone for argumentation theory for nearly two decades and which organises argumentation patterns by the conjunction of a goal (what the argument is for) and a basis (what the argument rests on). The four-cell split we use on ST3 (practical / epistemic × internal / external) is operationalised by the lab task description [Heinrich et al. 2026]; its conceptual antecedents are the argumentation-profile framing of Macagno [2022] and the broader scheme catalogue of Walton, Reed, and Macagno [2008]. The crossing reflects whether the speaker is arguing about what to do or what to believe, and whether their warrant comes from the speaker's own resources or from an external source. Jo et al. [2021] is, to our knowledge, the cleanest NLP-on-Walton-schemes prior work and the only one whose label set overlaps non-trivially with ST3; the shelf of comparable NLP work is thin, which is part of what makes the task interesting and also part of what means a system designer cannot transfer published model recipes directly.

## 2.3 Encoder versus LLM trade-offs

For short-text classification with a few hundred labelled examples per class, the literature offers both encoder fine-tuning and decoder LLM prompting as viable starting points. On the encoder side, RoBERTa [Liu et al. 2019] remains a strong default, and DeBERTa-v3 [He et al. 2023] and ModernBERT [Warner et al. 2024] have reported gains over RoBERTa on a range of GLUE-style and long-context benchmarks. The Qwen2.5 and Qwen3 model families [Qwen Team 2024; Qwen Team 2025] make modern decoder LLMs usable at 3B-32B parameter scales, and LoRA / QLoRA [Hu et al. 2022; Dettmers et al. 2023] make supervised fine-tuning tractable on a single consumer GPU. Chain-of-thought supervision [Wei et al. 2022] has become the standard reasoning-elicitation technique for these models. The literature does not, to our reading, settle the encoder-vs-LLM question for small-data fallacy classification — which is why the comparison in Section 5 and the discussion in Section 6 were worth running rather than assuming.

## 2.4 The Touché lab

Touché has run continuously since 2020 and has progressively shifted its scope from argument retrieval to full argumentation systems. Editions 2022 and 2023 [Bondarenko et al. 2022; Bondarenko et al. 2023] concentrated on argument retrieval and causal-evidence retrieval; the 2024 and 2025 editions [Kiesel et al. 2024; Kiesel et al. 2025] broadened to argumentation-system evaluation. The 2026 edition [Heinrich et al. 2026] is the first to feature fallacy detection as a dedicated lab task with the three-subtask structure we describe in Section 3.
